Know Before You Call

Some Homes Require a Panel Upgrade

Many homes built before 2000 have 100-amp electrical panels which may not support Level 2 EV chargers. If your panel is full or underpowered, a licensed electrician may recommend upgrading to a 200-amp service panel.

This is a common and straightforward upgrade — our electricians handle the permit, utility coordination, and inspection from start to finish.

💡 Typical panel upgrades range from $2,500 – $6,000 depending on the home, utility provider, and local permit requirements.

Savings & Incentives

Know What You May Qualify For

Available incentives depend on where you live and your utility provider. Programs change frequently — we walk you through what's currently active for your specific location so you don't miss anything you may be eligible for.

PA

Pennsylvania Homeowners (PECO Service Area)

There is currently no PECO residential EV charger installation rebate. PA homeowners may still qualify for the federal EV charger tax credit — see below. PECO does offer a separate $50 EV driver reporting credit for customers who notify PECO they purchased an EV.

Up to
$1,500

New Jersey Homeowners (PSE&G Make-Ready)

PSE&G residential customers in Burlington, Camden, and Gloucester counties may qualify for a make-ready incentive of $50–$1,500 per charger toward wiring, permits, and panel upgrade costs. Eligibility and amount depend on the project scope. Note: PSE&G's off-peak charging credit program is being phased out (no new applicants as of January 13, 2026) — this does not affect the make-ready incentive.

30%

Federal Tax Credit (PA & NJ)

The federal Alternative Fuel Vehicle Refueling Property Credit (IRS Form 8911) may cover up to 30% of qualifying costs (up to $1,000) for eligible home charger installations placed in service by June 30, 2026. Eligibility depends on whether your property is in a qualifying census tract and your individual tax situation — confirm with a tax advisor. We provide itemized documentation to support your filing.

Varies

Manufacturer Credits

We track active installation offers from Tesla, Ford, GM, and Rivian to ensure you aren't leaving money on the table at the time of purchase.

0%

NJ Sales Tax

As a licensed contractor, we ensure your installation qualifies as a "Capital Improvement," meaning you pay $0 in sales tax on the labor for your project.
