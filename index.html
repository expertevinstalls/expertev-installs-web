Final pricing depends on electrical panel capacity, wiring distance, and installation complexity. A licensed electrician will provide a firm written quote before any work begins.

Know Before You Call

Some Homes Require a Panel Upgrade

Many homes built before 2000 have 100-amp electrical panels which may not support Level 2 EV chargers. If your panel is full or underpowered, a licensed electrician may recommend upgrading to a 200-amp service panel.

This is a common and straightforward upgrade — our electricians handle the permit, utility coordination, and inspection from start to finish.

💡 Typical panel upgrades range from $2,500 – $6,000 depending on the home, utility provider, and local permit requirements.
